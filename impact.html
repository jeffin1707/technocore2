Asteroid Impact Information

Discover historical asteroid impact events and their effects on Earth.

Tunguska Event

Year: 1908

An asteroid or comet exploded in the atmosphere above Siberia, flattening 2,000 km² of forest.

Chelyabinsk Meteor

Year: 2013

A 20-meter meteor exploded over Chelyabinsk, Russia, injuring 1,500 people due to shattered glass.

Chicxulub Impact

Year: 66 Million Years Ago

Believed to have caused the extinction of the dinosaurs by creating massive global climate changes.

Vredefort Crater

Year: 2.023 Billion Years Ago

The largest verified impact crater on Earth, located in South Africa, formed by a massive asteroid collision.

Wilkes Land Crater

Year: 250 Million Years Ago

Located beneath Antarctica, it may be linked to the Permian-Triassic extinction event.

Campo del Cielo

Year: ~4,000–5,000 Years Ago

A meteorite shower in Argentina, leaving numerous iron meteorites and craters.

Hoba Meteorite

Year: ~80,000 Years Ago

The largest known intact meteorite on Earth, weighing over 60 tons, located in Namibia.

Allende Meteorite

Year: 1969

A carbonaceous chondrite meteorite fell in Mexico, providing insights into the early solar system.

Chegem Crater

Year: ~2.2 Million Years Ago

Located in the North Caucasus, Russia, a significant impact crater with a diameter of ~1.5 km.

Morokweng Crater

Year: ~145 Million Years Ago

A large meteorite impact crater in South Africa, about 70 km in diameter.

Moon Impact Event (Tycho Crater)

Year: ~108 Million Years Ago

A massive asteroid created the prominent Tycho Crater on the Moon, 85 km in diameter.

Sudbury Basin

Year: ~1.85 Billion Years Ago

One of the largest known impact structures, located in Ontario, Canada, ~250 km wide.

Peekskill Meteorite

Year: 1992

A meteorite fell in Peekskill, New York, famously damaging a car and widely witnessed by the public.

Mbale Meteorite

Year: 1992

A witnessed fall in Uganda, recovered soon after impact with minimal damage.

Other Recorded Events

Many smaller asteroids and meteors impact Earth regularly, most burning up in the atmosphere.
